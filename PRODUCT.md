## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are small to medium-sized business owners and their staff working in trades and construction. They are often time-pressed, moving between desk work and job sites, and need to understand work status quickly without digging through clutter. The product should support both leadership and field-adjacent staff with a workflow that feels organized, direct, and dependable.

The core job to be done is to give trades teams a Linear-like way to track and move work forward without feeling like generic startup software or bloated back-office tooling. The interface should help users see what matters, update work fast, and stay aligned with minimal friction.

### Brand Personality

The brand should feel slick, intuitive, and simple. It should project competence and polish without becoming flashy, precious, or trendy. The emotional goal is confidence: users should feel like the system is sharp, easy to trust, and always one step ahead of the messiness of daily operations.

The tone should stay grounded and professional for trades businesses. It must not feel childish, toy-like, or overly complex. It also should not lean into cluttered enterprise heaviness; clarity and restraint matter as much as polish.

### Aesthetic Direction

The product should borrow the ethos of Linear more than its literal visuals: calm precision, strong hierarchy, fast scanning, and a sense that every element earned its place. Light mode is the primary experience for now. Because the audience often works in practical, daylight-first environments, the interface should feel crisp and usable in bright conditions rather than optimized around dark-mode theatrics.

Since the app is built with shadcn/ui, the design should respect that foundation while pushing it toward a more distinctive, premium, trade-appropriate expression. Avoid default-looking shadcn token choices, playful color stories, or overly decorative SaaS patterns. Favor grounded neutrals, disciplined accent usage, durable-feeling surfaces, and layouts that feel efficient rather than busy.

### Design Principles

1. Design for fast jobsite scanning. Important information should be readable at a glance, with strong hierarchy, obvious next actions, and very low visual noise.
2. Keep polish tight and restrained. The product should feel premium through spacing, typography, rhythm, and finish rather than flashy effects or decorative excess.
3. Make complexity feel absent. Even when workflows grow richer, the interface should present them in a way that feels intuitive, calm, and easy to move through.
4. Stay grounded in the trades context. Visual choices should feel sturdy, practical, and professional, not childish, whimsical, or startup-generic.
5. Hold a high accessibility bar. Default to strong contrast, keyboard-friendly interaction, clear states, and respectful reduced-motion behavior.
